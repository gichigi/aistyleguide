Content Style Guide

Brand X Voice & Writing Rules

Updated 04 May 2025

How to Use This Document

This document outlines the rules for brand voice, spelling, grammar, and formatting across all content channels. Anyone writing and publishing content for NatWest should follow these guidelines.

Overview

Brand X speaks in a confident, helpful and slightly playful tone. This guide captures our core voice traits and 25 essential writing rules so every writer can deliver copy that feels unmistakably Brand X.

General Guidelines

Short‑form content

  • ➞ Be brief: Aim for 10 words or less per sentence and 2-3 sentences per parahraph.
  • ➞ Be an authority: sound confident and optimistic; show you know what you’re talking about.
  • ➞ Write like a human: use everyday language and avoid jargon.

Long‑form content

  • ➞ Humanise the story: start with why it matters now, then show the solution.
  • ➞ Use line breaks & white space: keep paragraphs to two or three sentences to improve readability.
  • ➞ Break up with sub‑heads: draft first, add headings where they help scanning; strong headings keep readers engaged.
  • ➞ Use bullet lists: for three or more points so ideas stand out.
  • ➞ Use numbers thoughtfully: the brain notices numerals; lead with key figures but don’t overload the reader.

25 Core Writing Rules

  1. Use active voice

    Active voice keeps sentences strong and direct.

    ✅ Right: "The team shipped the feature."

    ❌ Wrong: "The feature was shipped by the team."

  2. Keep sentences under 20 words

    Short sentences boost comprehension and mobile readability.

    ✅ Right: "Upload the file, then share the link."

    ❌ Wrong: "You should consider uploading your document and subsequently distributing the corresponding hyperlink to relevant stakeholders."

  3. Write in second person

    Speak directly to the reader using "you."

    ✅ Right: "You can customise colours."

    ❌ Wrong: "Users can customise colours."

  4. Spell out numbers one to nine

    Except for stats or UI labels.

    ✅ Right: "We offer three plans."

    ❌ Wrong: "We offer 3 plans."

  5. Use British English spelling

    Aligns with Brand X’s global roots.

    ✅ Right: "Organise", "Colour"

    ❌ Wrong: "Organize", "Color"

  6. One idea per paragraph

    Prevents cognitive overload.

    ✅ Right: Two short paragraphs covering two ideas.

    ❌ Wrong: One wall‑of‑text paragraph covering six ideas.

Warning

Avoid ALL‑CAPS sentences—they feel like shouting and trip screen‑readers.
